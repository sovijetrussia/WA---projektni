Asus has put together an undeniably good product once again. The Geforce GTX 960 STRIX OC is one of the coolest running graphics cards money can buy today, and by extension it’s also one of the quietest. The already overclocked GPU and memory perform very well out of the box. Anyone with a GTX 960 on their short-list would be wise to consider this card heavily.

Typically, a reference design would be used for comparison. Since there isn't one, though, we used a Zotac GTX 960 clocked as closely to reference as possible, since it has a similar power target compared to standard designs. We’ll also test it against this card at stock speeds, in addition to an EVGA sample.
The tests on our list include thermal capabilities, power consumption, acoustic levels and overclocking potential.

When we were testing noise levels even under full load after 10 minutes, the meter never registered anything higher than 35dB. Suffice to say this is one quiet graphics card.

Maximum power draw of this card was about 144W while average wobbled around 100W and with low beind just 7W.

This card allows for a great overclock if you know what you are doing thanks to its thermals.
